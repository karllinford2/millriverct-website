The Mission

The Mill River Estuary Foundation is being formed exclusively for charitable and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3). Its purpose is to preserve, protect, restore, and beautify this unique stretch of waterway and its environs — from Perry’s Mill Pond downstream through upper Southport Harbor — for the benefit of the public and for future generations.

The waters where the Mill River meets Southport Harbor belong to no one — and yet to all of us

Our Focus

The Water

Where Long Island Sound’s tides meet the Mill River’s fresh flow, a brackish estuary sustains rich plant, marine, and bird life. Keeping its water levels balanced is vital to that future — which is why MREF aims to maintain them by restoring the historic Tide Mill Dam.

Aquatic Life

Often called the “nurseries of the sea,” estuaries are where countless creatures spawn and mature. MREF aims to ease passage for migrating herring, alewives, and eels, and to support blue crabs, diamondback terrapins, and the many species that keep Mill River’s waters diverse and healthy.

The Banks

Native marsh grasses and seagrasses filter sediment and runoff before it reaches the harbor. By controlling invasives, planting more natives, and reducing sedimentation in the Upper Harbor, MREF works to protect the banks and help oysters, marine life, and recreation thrive there.

Avian Life

The estuary’s rich ecosystem is home to many notable birds — Bald Eagles, Ospreys, Cormorants, Swans, Blue Herons and Egrets, along with countless waterfowl, ducks, and geese. As the health and diversity of the waterway improve, so will the bird life that depends on it.

Our First Priority

For more than three centuries the Tide Mill Dam has regulated the flow of water from the Mill River into Southport Harbor. Years of disrepair — worsened by the ice floes of winter 2025/2026 — have dropped water levels and let sediment accumulate. Restoring this historic structure is our first and most urgent undertaking.

A Call to Join Us

We are only stewards of this special place, holding it in trust for those who come after us. Your support will help restore the dam, protect this living estuary, and preserve a treasured link to our past.
